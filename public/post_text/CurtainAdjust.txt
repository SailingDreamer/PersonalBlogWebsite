<b>Problem Statement</b>:
Waking up feeling refreshed is a large challenge for most. The sun helps slowly ready our bodies for the long day ahead, reinforcing the circadian rhythm and sleep cycle. Unfortunately, many people who wake before sunrise lack his natural system, leaving them tired and lethargic for the rest of the day.

<b>Mission</b>:
Build a natural waking device that can function as a room light and an incrementally-increasing sun-mimicing wake up system in the morning.

<b>Requirements</b>:

* Can slowly increase intensity on an LED strip with 50V MOSFET transistors.
* ESP32 running the system can connect to the local time server through WI-FI and request information regarding time of day to ensure the sunrise cycle is on time.
* Can also function as a regular room LED light, adjusting from white to red light after 6:00PM to ease the user into sleep.
* ESP32 system can connect to multiple Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) systems for future projects (See below: room vent fan controller, remote light switch, and curtain control)

<b>Prototype</b>:
Since this project was electronics-heavy, I started by experimenting with NPN transistors and ensuring the ESP32 system could accurately control the respective red, green, and blue (RGB) channels allocated via the C++ program.

<b>Setup</b>:

Closer demonstration of the 3D printed Stepper Motor interface for adjusting the curtain lines. The system uses two 1:2 reduction gears, giving the drive reduction a total of 1:4 reduction between the stepper shaft and the pulleys controling the string.
